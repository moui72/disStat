4 Results

This section reports various descriptions and analyses of the recordings obtained. The reported results include the effect of speech act (declarative/D vs. interrogative/Q) and PP2 status (argument/Arg vs. modifier/Mod) on the location of prosodic breaks, as well as on time spent studying a sentence between readings, which I call inter-reading time (IRT). In order to evaluate the extent to which participants completed the protocol as intended, i.e. began to read immediately for Reading 1 as opposed to producing a studied reading in Reading 2, the delay for which a sentence is displayed before a participant begins to read it is compared for Reading 1 (R1 delay) vs. Reading 2 (R2 delay). The prosodic patterns for participants with especially fast and especially slow R1 delays are presented as a way of investigating the extent to which individual differences might impact those patterns, and as a further exploration of the success of the protocol instructions in producing the intended behavior. An interesting auxiliary finding regarding the effect of speech act in inflating IRT in general (regardless of PP2 status; indeed, even for filler items) is also reported.

Some of the expected 3072 (32 participants x 48 items (16 experimental and 32 filler) x 2 readings each) recordings were missing due to technical issues during data collection, including some of the recordings of experimental items. Data were also excluded if either recording from a pair of recordings (Reading 1 and Reading 2) was missing (there were 9 incomplete pairs excluded) because without the other member, it is difficult to determine the extent to which the protocol was executed as intended (i.e. the extent of preview for Reading 1 vs. Reading 2).

4.2 Prosodic break patterns
